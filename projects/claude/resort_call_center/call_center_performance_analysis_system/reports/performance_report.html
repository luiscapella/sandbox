Coaching Playbook

Golden Rules (what top agents do on every call)

1. Open warm and by name — set the tone in the first 10 seconds.
2. Discover WHY they're calling (occasion, group size, dates) before pitching anything.
3. Present the right suite with specifics — not just prices.
4. Add one upsell or package before asking for the sale.
5. Handle every objection with a concrete alternative, not silence.
6. Close assumptively — 'Let me get that booked right now.'
7. End with genuine excitement — customers remember how you made them feel.

Priority Gaps to Close

Value Framing — Gap: +58.7
Sell the experience before quoting the price.
  • Always describe what's included before mentioning the price.
  • Use social proof: 'Our guests consistently say it's the highlight of their stay.'
  • Connect features directly to the customer's reason for calling.
Script example:
Instead of: 'The suite is $450.' → 'The Ocean Bliss Suite starts at $450 and includes champagne on arrival, a private balcony, and a couples' spa credit — it's our most popular anniversary package.'
Rapport Building — Gap: +46.0
Establish a human connection before selling anything.
  • Ask about the occasion or purpose of the trip early.
  • React emotionally to what customers share ('How special!').
  • Mirror the customer's tone and energy.
Script example:
Customer: 'It's our anniversary.' → 'Oh, how special! Congratulations — let me make sure we get you something truly memorable.'
Warmth & Niceness — Gap: +44.2
Be warm, human, and personal on every call.
  • Use the customer's name at least twice.
  • Acknowledge emotional context: anniversaries, family trips, special occasions.
  • Open with energy: 'How can I make your day amazing?'
  • Close with genuine excitement: 'I'm so excited for you!'
Script example:
Instead of: 'Ok, what dates.' → 'Wonderful! What dates are you thinking, [Name]? I'd love to help you find the perfect fit.'
